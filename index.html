Development of a Novel Tritium Measurement Device and Continuous Monitoring System

This can be used not only for tritium, but also for carbon-14, phosphorus-32, and sulfur-35.

1. Introduction

Ever-Lab 151 focuses on developing advanced measurement technologies for tritium (HTO), a topic of significant scientific and societal interest since the Fukushima Daiichi accident. Our goal is to create a new tritium detection device and a continuous monitoring system that surpass the limitations of existing methods.

2. What is Tritium?

Tritium (³H, ³T) is a radioactive isotope of hydrogen with a half-life of 12.3 years and very low beta energy (18.6 keV max). In nature, it exists almost exclusively as HTO (tritiated water).

3. Conventional Tritium Quantification

The standard method uses an emulsified scintillator (“cocktail”) and a liquid scintillation counter (LSC). Beta particles excite fluor molecules, producing scintillation light detected by the LSC.

4. Limitations of the Conventional Method

  • Chemical quenching caused by salts (e.g., seawater)
  • Radioactive organic waste requiring special disposal
  • No continuous measurement due to batch-only operation

5. Our Proposal: PS Thin-Film Tritium Detector

We propose a new device that enables scintillation without direct contact between HTO and fluorescent molecules, that is, a device that laminates a plastic scintillator (PS) thin film fabricated by a casting method.

  • Can be used with general-purpose LSC
  • No quenching from salts
  • Washable and reusable
  • Compatible with continuous monitoring

7. Advantages of PS Thin Films

Compared with pellet-type PS, thin films guide scintillation light toward the film edges, improving detection efficiency.

8. Plasma Surface Treatment

PS films are naturally hydrophobic. Low-temperature plasma treatment modifies the surface to become hydrophilic without affecting bulk properties.
